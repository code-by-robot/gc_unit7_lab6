What's with your name?
I spent a semester playing women's rugby in college. My old/legal name was three syllables and I wanted something shorter that could be yelled. I thought Rob was cool. Cue gender feelings.

Originally, I said Rob was short for Robert, but I was so used to being the only one with my name in places that having to best every single Rob, Bob, Bobby and Robby in hand-to-hand combat was daunting. (That's what you have to do when you have the same name as someone. I don't make the rules.)

I also felt disconnected from Robert as a name and chose the non-binary noun name of Robot because 1) R.O.B. from Nintendo was cool and 2) Robot is a really cool name.
What did you do before coding?
Not much, really.

From childhood to my sophomore year of college, I was focused on classical music, mainly on viola and mainly in orchestral settings. Chamber music was ok, but people actually showed up to the big ensemble concerts. During my freshman year of college, I started taking engineering classes for fun.

I switched majors to nuclear engineering, which is what my bachelor's is in. I worked at a nuclear plant for almost five years. In 2020, I met my wife online through a transgender-only Discord server. She helped me realize 1) I was autistic as hell, 2) I was being treated really poorly at my job (i.e. being placed on unpaid leave for over a month after requesting ADA accommodations, only to have my requests denied). I left my job to move across the country and live with her.

Because of the treatment I faced at my old plant, I decided to leave the nuclear industry and make a career change to software engineering. It helps that my wife's a senior software engineer so I can go to her for help and tips.
